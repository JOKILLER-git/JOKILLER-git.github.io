<h1 style="font-size:32px;margin-bottom:0.5em">Personal Statement</h1>

![](/img/Images/4.png)

<div style="font-size:18px">

The game scenes reflecting elegant architecture always fascinate me, conveying hope and faith that convince me that noble aspects of humanity exist. It is my ardent passion and aspiration to delve into this captivating medium for future studies.

During my undergraduate learning in Software Engineering at Beijing Institute of Technology, I have built a solid foundation in programming languages and practical skills of software development, project management, and computer graphics and image processing. In addition, I took relevant courses, such as Digital Art Design and Game Design and Development, to become familiar with game design theories. For the final project, I created a third-person runner game demo based on the mythology of King Arthur, including narratives, character design, world views and game progression systems. It gave me a great sense of achievement and valuable perspectives as a game designer. 

Driven by my enthusiasm, I took the initiative to join Moyu Game Club in my first year. We held seminars on Unity, Aseprite, and pixel design, and organised a Game Jam. Since the following summer in 2024, I have been a member of Maple Leaf Studio, founded by an alumnus. Among our creations, Realm of Ink, a Roguelite game, was officially launched on Steam. I helped draft scripts alongside technical personnel, while Leap Studio provided stunning visuals. Through this collaborative experience, I gained a deeper understanding of the different roles in game design.

From June to August 2025, I worked on our ambitious project Forbidden City Sega, which deepened my understanding of the interdisciplinary expertise required of a game planner. This sandbox game aims to give players freedom to explore amid the splendid architecture. Although a 2D top-down approach would have been more feasible, we eventually adopted 3D perspectives, which posed challenges with camera occlusion, longer configuration times, and asset import between software. For instance, the turns of the corners of the Forbidden City's walls desired a natural effect, which was difficult to achieve. It made me aware of the designer's responsibilities to coordinate and synthesise.

Regarding map design, the technical team initially delivered an editing system that did not integrate well with Unity. It led to unexpected errors during the release of the first demo. Facing piles of coding documents, we managed to fix the problems in time. In retrospect, it occurred to me that the problem stemmed from overly large files resulting from implementation methods. As a planner, I needed to both understand programming and communicate effectively with the technical department.

I am determined to keep exploring world-building through level and gameplay design. With a robust research underpinning, the MA in Game Design at the University of the Arts London appeals to me deeply. It will equip me with systemic design thinking for advanced game mechanics and system design. In terms of career, I aim first to accumulate experience across planning roles, and progress to a lead designer or producer. Ultimately, I aspire to establish a studio focused on indie games to bring joyful, memorable experiences through thoughtful design.
